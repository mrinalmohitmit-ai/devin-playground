The best mornings are usually the quiet ones. No open inbox, no background tab avalanche, no rush to be useful before the coffee is ready. Just a few clean minutes where attention is still available.

## What helps most

I keep the first hour intentionally small:

- make coffee or tea
- open a notebook instead of a feed
- sketch the one thing that matters
- begin with a paragraph, not a plan

> Momentum is easier to trust when you can see it happen in one page.

A writing routine does not need to be cinematic. It just needs to be repeatable.

### A tiny example

```ts
const focus = ['one tab', 'one task', 'one clear next step'];

export function morning() {
  return focus.join(' · ');
}
```

If you want more structure, try a simple three-line ritual: what I know, what I am unsure about, and what I will do next. That is often enough to turn fog into movement.
