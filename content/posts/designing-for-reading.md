A blog can feel welcoming without feeling busy. The difference is often less about adding features and more about removing friction.

## The essentials

Good reading experiences usually share a few traits:

1. A clear hierarchy
2. Comfortable line length
3. Enough contrast in both themes
4. Links that look like links
5. Motion that supports, not distracts

For long-form content, I prefer generous spacing and a typographic system that respects the shape of paragraphs. It makes the page feel slower in the best possible way.

[Read more about readability guidelines](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tips/writing/).

### Checklist for a post card

- title first
- short excerpt
- metadata that answers the obvious questions
- a single action

When the interface is quiet, the article gets louder.
