Constraints are useful because they reduce the number of believable excuses. If the scope is small, the feedback is fast. If the system is simple, the problem is visible.

## Why limits help

They force decisions around what matters most:

- What is the first value the user gets?
- What can be delayed until later?
- What is truly unique here?

> A small product can still feel generous when the core loop is honest.

That is one of the reasons I like static content sites: the architecture encourages clarity. You write the post, shape the page, and ship the idea without negotiating with a database.

### The hidden win

Fewer moving parts usually means fewer failures, but the deeper benefit is editorial discipline. Every section has to earn its place.
